Lake Como is not only about scenery and villas — it's also a serious food destination. Larian cuisine blends mountain and lakeside tradition with Lombard and Alpine influences, creating truly unique dishes. Here's our selection of the best places to eat.

## The Cuisine of the Lake

Before diving into the restaurants, a word on tradition: the signature dishes of the area include **missultin** (dried agone fish), **perch risotto**, **polenta uncia** with melted butter and cheese, and **pizzoccheri** from Valtellina. For dessert, the **panettone comasense** and chestnuts are unmissable in autumn.

## Where to Eat in Bellagio

Bellagio has an excellent food scene. Lakeside restaurants offer spectacular views, but for the most authentic experience head into the alleys of the historic centre. Look for trattorias offering tasting menus with local lake dishes — it's the best way to understand the food of the area.

**Tip**: Always book ahead, especially in summer. Bellagio is extremely popular and the best tables fill up fast.

## Where to Eat in Varenna

Varenna is more intimate and authentic than Bellagio. Its lakeside restaurants have direct views over the water, ideal for a romantic lunch. Many serve the catch of the day, freshly fished.

## Where to Eat in Como City

The centre of Como offers a wider variety: traditional osteria, pizzeria, and fine-dining restaurants. For a quick lunch before your boat tour, look for the **trattorias in the old town** near the Broletto.

## Wine Pairings

The Lake Como area sits at the gateway to **Valtellina**, one of Italy's most interesting wine regions. Nebbiolo-based reds (Sassella, Grumello, Inferno) pair perfectly with meat dishes and aged cheeses. For freshwater fish, try a white Lugana or a Franciacorta.
