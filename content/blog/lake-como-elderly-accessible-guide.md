Lake Como is a remarkably accessible destination for elderly travellers — provided you approach it the right way. The lake itself presents no physical barriers. The challenge, as with any Italian destination, is the steep terrain of some villages and the crowds on the public ferries. A private taxi boat solves both problems entirely.

## Why the Private Boat Works So Well for Elderly Guests

Public ferries are crowded, noisy and require standing in queues, navigating gangways and competing for seating. A private taxi boat is the opposite: we come to you, we help you board at your own pace, and you sit in comfort for the entire journey.

The boat departs directly from Como city. There is no travel to a distant marina, no long walk, no stairs. Boarding is assisted and the seating is comfortable. The skipper maintains a calm, steady pace and stops whenever you want.

## What to See Without Extensive Walking

One of the great advantages of the boat tour is that you see the most beautiful things on Lake Como from the water — where they are meant to be seen. The terraced gardens of Villa Balbianello, the facades of Villa d'Este, the colourful reflections of Varenna's harbour: all of these are best experienced from the lake, not from a crowded path.

If you would like a land stop, we recommend Bellagio's lower lakefront — flat, paved and very manageable — or Varenna's waterside promenade. Both are accessible without significant climbing.

## Practical Considerations

We recommend booking a morning departure, when the lake is calmest and the light is at its best. The 2-hour or 3-hour tour is typically ideal — long enough to see the main highlights, not so long as to be tiring. Bring a light layer as the lake breeze can be cool even in summer.
